## Operator "solutions" — what they sell beyond connectivity

Modern operators are diversifying well past voice/data:

| Solution area | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Mobile money / fintech** | M-Pesa (Safaricom), Orange Money, Airtel Money, MTN MoMo — banking the unbanked |
| **Enterprise & B2B** | Private 5G networks, IoT connectivity, SD-WAN, cloud, cybersecurity services |
| **Fixed broadband & TV** | Fiber to the home, IPTV, bundles ("quad play") |
| **Cloud & edge** | Telco edge compute (MEC), hyperscaler partnerships (AWS/Azure/GCP in the network) |
| **Wholesale / MVNO enablement** | Renting network capacity to virtual operators |
| **Digital services** | Content, gaming, security, insurance add-ons |

## MNO vs MVNO

- **MNO** (Mobile Network Operator): owns spectrum + network (all above).
- **MVNO** (Mobile Virtual Network Operator): no own network — rents from an MNO and resells under its own brand (e.g. many budget/niche brands). Enabled by the operator's [BSS/wholesale systems](oss-bss.md).

## Industry bodies worth knowing

- **GSMA** — represents mobile operators worldwide; runs MWC, drives standards like eSIM and Open Gateway.
- **3GPP** — the body that actually standardizes 3G/4G/5G specs.
- **ITU** — UN agency; allocates spectrum, sets "IMT" requirements (what qualifies as 4G/5G).
- **TM Forum** — BSS/OSS standards ([oss-bss](oss-bss.md)).
